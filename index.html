About Me

I didn't start in tech. I started in a warehouse, worked my way up through data and operations roles and spent nearly two years in the NHS analysing workforce information. When I moved into my current role at One Advanced Ltd I was specifically looking for something closer to development, even if that meant VBA and building legal forms to start with. Every role I have had has come with something new to pick up and I got comfortable with that fairly early on.

Somewhere in those ten years I realised I wanted to actually build things, not just use them. So in 2022 I started a BSc in Web Design, Development and Analytics at Edge Hill University. I graduate in summer 2026. The area I have focused on most is accessibility: writing semantic HTML, testing with screen readers and making sure interfaces work for everyone without needing a workaround.

Outside of work and study I read a lot, hike when I can and snowboard. I picked snowboarding up as an adult, which gives you a fairly reliable reminder of what it feels like to know nothing. I tend to do better when there is something difficult to work towards, which is probably why switching careers at this point made sense.

I also crochet. It sounds unrelated, but the more I've thought about it, the more the two feel the same. Learning a pattern language, executing steps in a specific order, looping through the same logic until the shape emerges, and knowing that losing track of your place costs you the whole thing. That is the thread running through this site.

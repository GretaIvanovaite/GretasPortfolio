About Me

I didn't start in tech. I started in a warehouse, worked my way up through data and operations roles and spent nearly two years in the NHS analysing workforce information. When I moved into my current role at One Advanced Ltd I was specifically looking for something closer to development, even if that meant VBA and building legal forms to start with. Every role I have had has come with something new to pick up and I got comfortable with that fairly early on.

Somewhere along the way I realised I wanted to actually build things, not just use them. So in 2022 I started a BSc in Web Design, Development and Analytics at Edge Hill University. I graduate in summer 2026. The area I have focused on most is accessibility: writing semantic HTML and adjusting JavaScript to create accessible elements, testing with screen readers and making sure interfaces work for everyone without needing a workaround.

Outside of work and study I read a lot, hike when I can and snowboard. I picked up snowboarding as an adult, which gives you a good reminder of what it feels like to know nothing but I enjoy learning new things. I also tend to do better when there is something to work towards, which is probably why getting into tech at this point made sense.

I also crochet. It sounds unrelated, but the more I've thought about it, the more the two feel very similar. Learning a pattern language, executing steps in a specific order, looping through the same logic until the shape emerges, and knowing that missing a stitch or a bug can ruin your whole project. That is the thread running through this site.
